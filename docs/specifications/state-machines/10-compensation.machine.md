# Machine M10 — Compensation

*Registry: `registry.md`. Entity: `entities/13-compensation.md`. Lifecycle: Target Spec §12.10; F-17.*

> ### **A Compensation is a separate consequential effect. It receives NO privileged path — it uses the same Action Pipeline (M2), policy, brake, approval, checkpoint, grant (M3), verification, and audit as any effect.** The states below are the *compensation's own* lifecycle; its *execution* is a full M2/M3 run.

**1.** Compensation. **2.** Compensation. **3. Purpose.** Undo a `VERIFIED` effect that a later correction invalidated — through the full pipeline, never a fast path. **4. Aggregate/txn.** Own aggregate; ### **execution is a SEPARATE M2 Pipeline Instance.** **5. Owner.** ### **a human, from `REQUIRED`.** **6. Initial.** `REQUIRED`. **7. States.** the 6 (registry §4/M10). **8. Terminal.** `COMPLETED`. **9. Non-terminal human-owned.** `REQUIRED, COMPENSATION_FAILED, NOT_POSSIBLE`. **10. Recoverable.** `APPROVED, EXECUTING`. **11. Failure.** `COMPENSATION_FAILED` (non-terminal). **12. Expiry.** ### **NEVER.** **13. Unknown-outcome.** its executing pipeline may go `NEEDS_VERIFICATION` ⇒ `COMPENSATION_FAILED`.

## 15. Illegal transitions. ### **compensating an `UNKNOWN_OUTCOME` → REFUSED (CM-1r).** A compensating effect bypassing the checkpoint → ILLEGAL (CM-3 requires a full pipeline). A `COMPENSATION_FAILED` moved by a timer → ILLEGAL. A bulk-undo → ILLEGAL (each is individually gated). **16. Precedence.** refusal-on-unknown (CM-1r) precedes creation. **17. Concurrency.** `UNIQUE(tenant,original_effect_id) WHERE state!='NOT_POSSIBLE'` — one active compensation per invalidated effect; its executing pipeline has its own commit-once. **18. Version-check.** GR-13 in its executing pipeline. **19. Idempotency.** GR-4 + the unique index. **20. Retry.** ### **a failed compensation is NOT auto-retried — a human decides** (CM-5). **21. Replay.** GR-11 — the compensating effect, like any effect, is never produced by replay.
## 22. Cancellation. n/a once `REQUIRED` (the exposure exists). **23. Expiry.** never. **24. Reopening.** n/a. **25. Correction.** n/a. **26. Supersession.** n/a. **27. Compensation.** (self). **28. Human-approval.** CM-2 always. **29. Policy.** its pipeline evaluates step 6. **30. Brake.** ### **BLOCKED under an active brake** (a misbehaving system must not write "corrections"); an urgent compensation requires a human to NARROW the brake first.
## 31. Evidence/provenance. the invalidating correction carries a `decision_ref` or a deterministic rule id; never `MODEL_INFERRED`. **32. Events.** CM-* per registry. **33. Consumes.** `CorrectionInvalidatedAnEffect, HumanApproved, NoCompensatingActionExists, PipelineClosed/Failed/NEEDS_VERIFICATION, HumanEstablishedReality`. **34. Writes.** compensation row + (execution) a Pipeline Instance + outbox. **35. Txn.** each transition one commit; execution is a separate pipeline's txns. **36. Crash recovery.** a `REQUIRED`/`COMPENSATION_FAILED` compensation persists, human-owned; its executing pipeline recovers per M2 (crash after claim ⇒ `NEEDS_VERIFICATION` ⇒ `COMPENSATION_FAILED`). **37. Timeout.** none moves a non-terminal human-owned state. **38. Observability.** ### **`COMPENSATION_FAILED`/`NOT_POSSIBLE` are the most dangerous states (reality and projection are KNOWN to diverge) — loud, owned, exposure shown.** **39. Audit.** required/approved/executing/completed/failed/impossible with `reason` and `decision_ref`. **40. Security.** a compensation is an effect — full checkpoint; no bypass.
## 41. Acceptance. (a) compensating an unknown outcome refused; (b) execution passes the full pipeline; (c) money comp is `HUMAN_APPROVAL_REQUIRED`; (d) no bulk undo (N individually gated, aggregate exposure first); (e) `COMPENSATION_FAILED` never auto-resolves.
## 42. Adversarial. Traces 10 (compensation also unknown), 13 (correction invalidates effect).
## 43. Open validation. **V1** interaction (reopening may raise a compensation). Fail-closed: each human-approved. Not a block.
