## 15. Illegal transitions. ### **`AutoResolve` and ANY `TimerFired`-to-resolved ‚Üí ILLEGAL** (a clock knows nothing about freight). Resolution with neither `rule_id` nor `decision_ref` ‚Üí ILLEGAL. A model resolving ‚Üí ILLEGAL (GR-7). A consequential action proceeding on a field with an OPEN conflict ‚Üí ILLEGAL/blocked (GR-10). An ownerless Conflict ‚Üí ILLEGAL (owner at creation). **16. Precedence.** ### **while OPEN, the field is `conflicting` and BLOCKS every consequential transition on the entity (GR-10)** ‚Äî this precedence dominates all machines that read the field. **17. Concurrency.** `UNIQUE(tenant,entity_ref,field) WHERE state‚àà{RAISED,OPEN,ESCALATED}` ‚Äî a second detection attaches a party (CF-7), not a new conflict. **18. Version-check.** n/a (the Conflict itself); it *causes* consequential blocks elsewhere. **19. Idempotency.** GR-4 + the partial unique index. **20. Retry.** n/a. **21. Replay.** GR-11.
## 22. Cancellation. only if the **underlying disagreement disappears** (a party retracts) ‚Äî still an event, never silence. **23. Expiry.** never. **24. Reopening.** a resolved field that *later* attracts new conflicting evidence raises a **NEW** Conflict (CF-1) ‚Äî the prior resolution stands in history (GR-12). **25. Correction.** a resolution may *cause* a downstream claim correction (M6 IB-7). **26. Supersession.** n/a. **27. Compensation.** if resolution reveals a wrong completed effect ‚áí M10. **28. Human-approval.** an open Conflict on a material field voids/blocks the M4 approval. **29. Policy.** ### **RULE_VS_RULE: two conflicting standing rules ‚áí this Conflict ‚áí a human resolves; Neyma never picks a winner (GR-15).** **30. Brake.** conflict detection continues under a brake.
## 31. Evidence/provenance. each `parties[]` entry carries its `provenance_class`; `INFERRER_VS_OWNER` records that one party is `OWNER_ASSERTED`. **32. Events.** CF-* per registry. **33. Consumes.** `ConflictDetected, Acknowledged, DeterministicRuleApplies, HumanResolved, AgeThresholdCrossed`. **34. Writes.** conflict row + field-condition + outbox. **35. Txn.** raise+freeze one commit. **36. Crash recovery.** an open Conflict persists; the frozen field stays blocking until resolved. **37. Timeout.** CF-5 escalation only (never resolution). **38. Observability.** ### **conflict count should be near zero; a persistent conflict is an operational problem in the business ‚Äî surfacing it IS the product.** **39. Audit.** raise/open/escalate/resolve with parties and resolution basis. **40. Security.** ### **a Conflict is a security control ‚Äî an injected competing claim yields a FROZEN entity + a human's attention, not control.** Resolution requires a registered rule_id or an authenticated human decision_ref.
## 41. Acceptance. (a) open Conflict blocks all consequential actions on the entity; (b) no timer/model resolves; (c) resolution needs a rule_id or decision_ref; (d) inferrer-vs-owner raises rather than overwrites; (e) two conflicting rules block, not auto-merge.
## 42. Adversarial. Traces 6 (conflicting observation), 7 (inferrer-vs-owner), 13 (correction).
## 43. Open validation. **V5** (registered conflict-resolution rules). Fail-closed: no rule ‚áí every conflict to a human. Not a block.
