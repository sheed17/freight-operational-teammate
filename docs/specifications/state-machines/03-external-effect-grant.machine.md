# Machine M3 — External Effect / Effect Grant *(one row, one machine, eight states)*

*Registry: `registry.md`. Entities: `entities/03-external-effect.md`, `entities/04-effect-grant.md`. Lifecycle: Target Spec §12.3, §19.6; ADR-004 §3.2 (SD-2 resolution).*

> ### **ONE `effect_grants` row. ONE machine. EIGHT states. NOT split into two machines and NOT two commit-key namespaces.** The **capability aspect** writes `{GRANTED, CLAIMED, EXPIRED_UNCLAIMED, REVOKED}`; the **outcome aspect** continues from `CLAIMED` into `{ATTEMPTED, VERIFIED, FAILED, UNKNOWN_OUTCOME}`. `CLAIMED` is the single join. **`REVOKED` is DISTINCT from `EXPIRED_UNCLAIMED`** (aligning to ADR-004 §3.2 over spec §12.3's narrative fold).

**1.** External Effect / Effect Grant. **2.** the `effect_grants` row. **3. Purpose.** Single-use, checkpoint-derived capability to touch the world once, plus the durable, honest record of what happened. **4. Aggregate/txn.** Own aggregate; **minted in ONE txn with the Checkpoint Witness + M2 `GRANTED`; claimed in ONE txn (the CAS) with M2 `CLAIMED` + M4 `CONSUMED` + `EffectAttempted`; verify+record in ONE txn.** **5. Owner.** system until `UNKNOWN_OUTCOME`, then a named human. **6. Initial.** `GRANTED` (at mint). **7. States.** the 8 (registry §4/M3). **8. Terminal.** `VERIFIED, FAILED, EXPIRED_UNCLAIMED, REVOKED`. **9. Non-terminal human-owned.** `UNKNOWN_OUTCOME`. **10. Recoverable.** `GRANTED, CLAIMED, ATTEMPTED`. **11. Failure.** `FAILED` (affirmative proof only, GR-5). **12. Expiry.** `EXPIRED_UNCLAIMED` (TTL, unclaimed). **13. Unknown-outcome.** `UNKNOWN_OUTCOME` (+ mandatory `unknown_reason`).

**Machine defaults:** ### **EF-2 (claim) and EF-3 (attempt/execute) are consequential.** `verification_outcome` is a separate field (registry). The commit-once index `UNIQUE(tenant,commit_key) WHERE state='CLAIMED'` holds at the claim instant; the durable through-life hold is M2's Layer-1 reservation.

## 15. Illegal transitions.
Mint without a witness → impossible (EF-1 guard). Two `CLAIMED` for one commit_key → impossible (unique index). `VERIFIED` without a healthy-match readback → ILLEGAL (EF-4 guard). `FAILED` without proof → ILLEGAL. Any timer on `UNKNOWN_OUTCOME` → ILLEGAL. `REVOKED`/`EXPIRED_UNCLAIMED`→ anything → ILLEGAL (terminal). Re-claiming a `CLAIMED`/`REVOKED`/`EXPIRED` grant → `ClaimRefused` (adapter inert).

## 16. Precedence. At `GRANTED`: `BrakeEngaged`/`PolicyVersionChanged`/`ApprovalRevoked` (EF-2r) and TTL (EF-2x) **take priority over** a claim — the CAS itself enforces this (it re-checks brake/policy/expiry). **17. Concurrency.** ### **The claim CAS is the sole serialization point for the effect** — atomic `GRANTED→CLAIMED`, exactly one winner. **18. Version-check.** `entity_versions` bound at mint (the SD-3 set), re-validated in the CAS via brake/policy versions; entity-version drift itself was caught at PL-8 (a stale entity ⇒ no witness ⇒ no mint). **19. Idempotency.** the CAS makes the claim idempotent; `EffectAttempted` is emitted exactly once (before the call) so a lost response is detectable. **20. Retry.** ### **NEVER re-claim a grant.** A retry is a **new grant** under the **same commit_key** (new EF-1 from a new PL-1) — and if the first attempt secretly committed, verification of the new attempt surfaces `OBSERVATION_CONFLICTING`; Layer-1 also blocks a concurrent second pipeline. **21. Replay.** GR-11 — ### **replay mints zero grants, claims none, produces zero `EffectAttempted`, touches the world zero times.**
## 22. Cancellation. ### **none after `CLAIMED`** — you cannot cancel that the world may have changed. Revocation applies only to `GRANTED` (EF-2r). **23. Expiry.** `GRANTED`→`EXPIRED_UNCLAIMED` (TTL); `UNKNOWN_OUTCOME` never expires. **24. Reopening.** n/a. **25. Correction.** the outcome, once `VERIFIED`/`FAILED`, is not re-established; a correction of an underlying binding raises M10. **26. Supersession.** n/a. **27. Compensation.** undoing a `VERIFIED` effect ⇒ M10 (its own gated effect).
## 28. Human-approval. `approval_id` bound at mint (DB CHECK when gate∈human); consumed in the claim CAS. **29. Policy.** `policy_version` bound at mint, re-validated in the CAS. **30. Brake.** ### **`brake_version` bound at mint, re-validated in the CAS — a brake between mint and claim makes the CAS match zero rows.** A claimed effect proceeds to verification (GR-16); the brake never converts it to failure.
## 31. Evidence/provenance. `VERIFIED_SUCCESS` requires a healthy positive-control readback matching the approved fingerprint; `external_record_ref` is `MODEL_EXTRACTED` evidence only, never an idempotency key (the TMS may renumber). **32. Events emitted.** EF-* per registry §5. **33. Events consumed.** `CheckpointPassed` (mint), `ClaimAttempted`, `AdapterReturned*`/`AdapterTimedOut`/`ProcessCrashed`, readback observations, `HumanEstablishedReality`, `LaterObservationProves`, `TimerFired` (TTL only). **34. Durable writes.** the ledger row (mint/claim/state) + co-committed M2/M4 + outbox. **35. Txn boundaries.** mint-txn, claim-CAS-txn, verify+record-txn — the three safety-critical commits. **36. Crash recovery.** after mint before claim ⇒ `GRANTED` expires ⇒ safe re-mint; ### **after claim ⇒ `UNKNOWN_OUTCOME`, never re-execute** (EF-3u); verify+record is atomic so that window does not exist. **37. Timeout.** TTL (EF-2x); **no timeout moves `UNKNOWN_OUTCOME`.** **38. Observability.** `UNKNOWN_OUTCOME` count Sev-1; ### **a `ClaimRefused` / orphan `EffectAttempted` with no matching claimed grant is a Sev-0 that auto-engages the brake** (§19.9 layer 5). **39. Audit.** every mint/claim/refusal/expiry/revoke/verify is an event; the grant is the permanent record of *why* an effect was permitted. **40. Security.** opaque+signed handle — neither is the control; a forged handle names no row (claim fails); a replayed handle hits an already-`CLAIMED` row (CAS fails); the adapter re-validates tenant/action/target against its own params (mismatch = Sev-0 confused deputy); agents can never hold a grant.

## 41. Acceptance criteria.
(a) mint requires a witness (type-level); (b) two claims ⇒ one wins; (c) forged/replayed handle fails; (d) wrong-target grant refused + Sev-0; (e) brake between mint and claim fails the claim; (f) blind readback ⇒ `OBSERVATION_UNAVAILABLE`, never failure; (g) `UNKNOWN_OUTCOME` never auto-resolves; (h) `REVOKED` ≠ `EXPIRED_UNCLAIMED` (both terminal, distinct events).
